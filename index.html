Matthew Corrigan
Principal

I help community legal centres, advocacy organisations, for-purpose organisations and industry associations across Australia build the evidence base for well-evidenced, politically credible law reform campaigns. I also help for-purpose organisations build the organisational systems and processes that turn good intentions into consistent, measurable results — not just compliance. I have over 20 years' multi-sector experience across government, private legal practice, the United Nations and international NGOs. Having led the Australian Law Reform Commission, the Queensland Law Reform Commission and worked with parliamentary committees, I know exactly the drivers that will inspire a reform body to effective action. I have developed law reform recommendations in some of the most complex areas of law and legal policy, including elder abuse, financial services law, class actions procedures and corporate crime.

“I know how a submission needs to be built, evidenced and pitched to be genuinely persuasive — because I've sat on the other side of that process.”

“I have rebuilt two underperforming public sector agencies from the ground up, embedding new governance, culture and systems that lifted staff engagement 18 points above the sector average and consistently improved performance against key deliverables.”
